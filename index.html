Understanding Binary Made Simple

Computers think in a language made of just two numbers: 0 and 1. This system is called binary. Each 0 or 1 is like a tiny switch—off (0) or on (1). By combining these switches, computers can store and process everything from pictures to text!

Imagine a series of light switches. When arranged in the right way, these switches create complex results. In the same way, computers use sequences of 0s and 1s to represent and manage all the information we see and use.

Don’t worry if it sounds complicated—binary is just the computer’s way of counting. You don't need to understand every detail to use it, but it's amazing how much can be achieved with just 0s and 1s!
